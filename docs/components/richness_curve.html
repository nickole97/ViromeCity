Virome × age

Viral richness across the human lifespan

How many kinds of virus live in the gut — with a few genuinely odd moments marked ✦.

✦ odd & interesting moments

  • ✦1At birth — nearly virus-free. The newborn gut starts almost empty; particles reach ~10ⁿ/g within a month.
  • ✦2~1 month — viruses from within. The first wave isn't caught from outside — they're prophages induced from the baby's own pioneer bacteria.
  • ✦3~4 months — milk shapes the virome. Viruses that infect human cells appear, and breastfeeding modulates which ones take hold.
  • ✦4Infancy — a virus that mirrors immunity. Anelloviruses (TTV) dominate, tracking an immature immune system, then fall as immunity matures.
  • ✦5Old age — the sleepers wake. Latent viruses reactivate, and virome shifts have been linked to cognitive decline.

Reading the lines: total richness climbs through infancy, dips in childhood, peaks in adulthood, then falls in old age. Underneath, eukaryotic viruses are high in infancy then settle low, while crAss-like phages keep climbing with age. Shape is schematic — from Gregory et al. 2020 (Cell Host & Microbe), Liang et al. 2020 (Nature) and Lim et al. 2015 (Nat. Medicine) — not exact values.
